Abdullah Al Mamun

Abdullah is a Research Analyst in the Markets, Trade and Institutions Division (MTID) of International Food Policy Research Institute (IFPRI) based in Washington DC. He analyzes household welfare models for poverty impact simulation of shocks such as weather, trade policy changes, and investment in Research and Development. He recently worked on a research study titled “El Nino Impacts and Trade Policy Responses on Grain Markets and Trade in Eastern and Southern Africa”. Abdullah designed and implemented a database in MySQL by processing household income and consumption expenditure survey data of 28 countries. He routinely uses R for advanced statistical analysis and MySQL for data processing. He is also skilled in other languages such as Python, Stata, GAMS etc. Prior to joining IFPRI, Abdullah worked at World Bank Group for seven years where he was involved in several evaluations that used randomized control trials (RCT) and other evaluation methods. He is a Bangladeshi citizen and holds B.Sc. in Statistics from Jahangirnagar University, Bangladesh and MS in Economics from University of Wisconsin-Madison, USA.
